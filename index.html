Li Acoustics is a specialist acoustic consultancy based in the United Kingdom, providing services in architectural acoustics, environmental noise assessment, and vibration control for residential, commercial, and performance-space projects.

Founded in 2018, the practice applies evidence-based acoustic engineering to produce environments that are both functionally correct and perceptually comfortable. Projects range from recording studios and auditoria to planning-stage noise surveys for mixed-use developments.

Background

Acoustics is the branch of physics concerned with the study of mechanical waves — including sound, infrasound, and ultrasound — and their interaction with matter. Applied architectural acoustics addresses how sound behaves within built spaces, influencing intelligibility, reverberation time (T60), and occupant comfort.

Li Acoustics was established to address a gap in practice between high-level acoustic modelling and on-site delivery. The consultancy works alongside architects, engineers, and contractors from RIBA Stage 1 through post-occupancy evaluation.

Services

Room acoustics

Room acoustic design encompasses the control of reverberation time, early reflections, and low-frequency room modes. Li Acoustics uses a combination of geometric ray-tracing software and wave-based finite-element methods to predict and optimise acoustic conditions before construction begins.

Noise & vibration control

Environmental noise assessments are conducted in accordance with BS 4142:2014 and PPG 24 guidance. Services include facade sound insulation design, mechanical plant noise surveys, and structural vibration measurement to BS 6472 criteria.

AV systems consultancy

For performance and corporate venues, Li Acoustics provides independent specification and procurement oversight for loudspeaker system design, room equalisation, and speech-intelligibility verification to STI standards.

Methodology

All projects follow a structured workflow: site survey and background noise measurement, parametric acoustic modelling, design-team coordination, specification writing, and post-completion measurement verification. Reports are produced to a standard suitable for planning authority submission where required.
